TOKEN BASED ODI IS REVOLUTIONARY

Token-based ODI is revolutionary because it creates a worldwide global collective without discrimination by uniting the most attractive network in the crypto world, the Ethereum blockchain with the insurance industry.

The blockchain makes it possible to create a global insurance market where individual premiums are pooled to cover catastrophes, while allowing price competition on premiums. BLOCKCHAIN TECHNOLOGY

Technical understandings

The blockchain manages data through the participants in the network and eliminates the need for companies to use the services of institutions such as banks, credit card companies or cloud services. Institutions that act as middlemen between people and create trust in the handling of business processes.

Paying bills or demanding payments for products and services can also take place without intermediaries through applications within the blockchain. One of these applications is smart contracts. These are web-based computer protocols that automate processes. You determine what is to be checked in a transaction and what consequences should result from it.

The contracts manage themselves and automatically implement processes such as identification, payment and performance. Supported by the integrity of the blockchain, transactions between two or more parties take place in a trustworthy manner, automatically and without the intervention of third parties. Broken transactions and thus the loss of revenue are a thing of the past with smart contracts.

Blockchain Works and Applications

Common protocols are currently Ethereum, Hyperledger Fabric and the R3 Corda platform, which specializes in the financial sector. Depending on the area of application, blockchain platforms are partly public and therefore freely accessible to everyone, and partly purely private for a predefined group of users. In both cases, all data is stored for all participants at the same time. That makes them forgery-proof.

In information technology one speaks of the "single point of truth", i.e. a parallel existence of contradicting data is almost impossible. With large numbers of participants, however, the blockchain becomes more cumbersome: At the moment, it still takes a lot of memory, a lot of energy and a lot of time before a transaction can be processed in a large public blockchain, as it has to be validated by each participant. However, it can be assumed that these problems will be resolved in the future.

How does it work?

ODI is a type of disability insurance that pays out if the insured is unable to work in a job that they are qualified for based on their education, experience, and age. Any occupation policy would not pay benefits if the insured could still work, even if it was at a lower-paying job. If you work in an injury-prone profession, you should definitely invest in disability insurance. Disability insurance is like life insurance. If your family doesn't rely on your income, then you probably don't need to buy .it works on solidarity principle.

What is Principle Of Solidarity?

The principle of solidarity is a socioethical and political concept that states that it is fair and just for members of society to share benefits and obligations equally. It entails contributions from Gross Salaries and is not a part of Taxes. Employers could also benefit from this if they recommended the scheme to their employees

What ODI Covers ?

There are a few specific conditions to be met upon beginning the insurance cover or in the event of an extension of the insurance cover. They include confirmation that What ODI Covers All insured persons are able to do their contractual daily work both mentally and physically No insured person has received a premium increase of more than 50% or a reduced or rejected benefit commitment during the previous 12 months under a different Occupational Disability Policy No insured person has received medical advice to stay away from work within the previous 60 days No insured person has reduced their working hours or changed their jobs during the previous 12 months due to accidents or illnesses (this applies to home jobs too), and No insured person was unable to attend work for more than 20 consecutive working days during the previous 12 months due to accidents or illnesses.
